No Man's Sky

No Man's Sky is a space exploration and survival game set in a procedurally generated universe of 18 quintillion planets. That number is not hyperbole -- every planet has its own terrain, weather, flora, and fauna, all generated from algorithms. You can fly seamlessly from a planet's surface into space, warp to another star system, and land on an entirely different world, all without loading screens.

The Redemption Story​

No Man's Sky is one of the most remarkable comeback stories in gaming. When it launched in August 2016, the gap between what was promised and what was delivered caused a massive backlash. The game felt empty -- no multiplayer, shallow survival, repetitive planets.

Instead of walking away, Hello Games went quiet and started shipping free updates. Not small patches -- massive, game-changing expansions:

  • Foundation (2016) -- Base building, freighters
  • Pathfinder (2017) -- Ground vehicles, permadeath mode
  • Atlas Rises (2017) -- 30-hour storyline, improved generation
  • NEXT (2018) -- Full multiplayer, third-person view, total visual overhaul
  • Beyond (2019) -- VR support, expanded multiplayer (the Nexus), cooking
  • Origins (2020) -- Dramatic terrain generation, volcanoes, sandworms
  • Frontiers (2021) -- Planetary settlements to manage
  • Sentinel / Outlaws / Endurance (2022) -- Combat overhaul, piracy, freighter bases
  • Waypoint / Interceptor / Echoes (2023) -- Customization, mech ships, robot companions
  • Omega / Worlds Part I & II (2024) -- Expedition rework, new biomes, water overhaul

Every single one of these updates was free. No paid DLC, no season pass. The game in 2026 is unrecognizable compared to its launch version.

Game Modes​

ModeDescription
NormalThe standard experience. Resource costs and hazard drain are moderate. Dying has minimal penalty. Best for first-time players.
SurvivalHarsher environments, scarcer resources, limited inventory. Getting off the starting planet is a genuine challenge.
PermadeathLike Survival but death is permanent. One life. The most intense way to play.
CreativeNo resource costs, no hazards, no combat. Unlimited building and exploration. Good for base designers and photographers.
CustomMix and match difficulty settings to your liking. Added in the Waypoint update.

Start with Normal to learn the systems. Survival and Permadeath are better appreciated once you understand what you are doing.

What You Actually Do​

The game does not force a single path, but here are the main activities:

Exploration​

The heart of the game. Each planet can surprise you -- lush paradise worlds, toxic wastelands, frozen hellscapes, or alien landscapes with floating islands and bioluminescent caves. Scanning fauna and flora earns credits, and uploading discoveries to the Atlas registers them with your name for other players to find.

Base Building​

Build anything from a small outpost to a sprawling multi-floor complex. Bases can include farms (grow resources), refiners (process materials), teleporters (fast travel back), and aesthetic decorations. You can build on any planet, on your freighter, or even underwater.

Ship Collecting​

There are multiple ship classes (Fighter, Explorer, Hauler, Shuttle, Exotic, Solar, Living Ship, Interceptor), each with different stats. Finding a perfect S-class ship in a color you love is its own endgame. Your freighter serves as a mobile base and fleet command center.

The Story​

There is actually a solid storyline (the Atlas Path and the Artemis quest line) that gradually reveals the nature of the simulation you exist in. It is philosophical and surprisingly well-written. Following it also teaches you the game's systems naturally.

Trading and Economy​

Each star system has an economy type and wealth level. You can trade goods between systems for profit, set up automated mining operations, or run a fleet of frigates on expeditions that bring back resources.

Multiplayer and the Nexus​

The Nexus is a shared social hub in the Anomaly station. You can see other players, pick up multiplayer missions, and join expeditions. Multiplayer lets you explore, build, and fight together. You can also encounter other players randomly in the shared universe, though space is so vast that unplanned meetings are rare.

Expeditions​

Time-limited seasonal events (roughly every 1-2 months) that give all players a fresh start with specific objectives. They tell a mini-story, introduce unique mechanics or challenges, and reward exclusive cosmetics and items. Completed expedition saves can be converted to a normal save afterward.

What Makes It Special​

No Man's Sky is a game about wonder. The scale is impossible to fully grasp -- you will never visit even a fraction of those 18 quintillion planets, and neither will anyone else. Some of the most memorable moments come from stumbling onto a planet that feels like no one has ever seen before (because no one has). The combination of scale, the freedom to go anywhere, and the relaxing loop of explore-gather-build-fly makes it a uniquely meditative experience.

It is also a testament to what happens when developers genuinely care about their game. Hello Games turned what could have been a cautionary tale into an inspiration.
