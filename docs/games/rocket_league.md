# Rocket League

Rocket League is car soccer -- literally. You drive a rocket-powered car and try to hit a giant ball into the opponent's goal. It went free-to-play in 2020 after years as a paid title, and the player base has stayed strong since. The concept is dead simple but the skill ceiling is absurdly high, which is what makes it compelling for hundreds (or thousands) of hours.

## The Basics

Matches are typically 5 minutes long (plus overtime if tied). The main competitive modes are **1v1**, **2v2**, and **3v3**. There are also casual playlists and rotating limited-time modes (Rumble, Hoops, Dropshot, Snow Day) that add power-ups or change the arena shape.

Every car hitbox falls into one of a few categories (Octane, Dominus, Plank, etc.), but the differences are subtle. Most players use **Octane** (well-rounded) or **Dominus** (flatter, good for flicks). Pick what feels right and stick with it.

## Competitive Ranks

The ranked ladder from bottom to top:

1. **Bronze** -- Learning basic controls
2. **Silver** -- Starting to hit the ball intentionally
3. **Gold** -- Ball chasing era, beginning aerials
4. **Platinum** -- Inconsistent aerials, rotation awareness
5. **Diamond** -- Faster play, wall shots
6. **Champion** -- Consistent aerials, team play, recoveries
7. **Grand Champion** -- Speed, precision, game sense
8. **Supersonic Legend** -- Top ~0.1% of the player base

Most players plateau around Platinum/Diamond. Breaking through requires deliberate practice, not just playing more games.

## Recommended Camera Settings

Camera settings make a huge difference in how you read the field. A good starting point:

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| **FOV** | 110 |
| **Distance** | 270 |
| **Height** | 100 |
| **Angle** | -4 |
| **Stiffness** | 0.40 - 0.50 |
| **Swivel speed** | 5.00+ |

These are close to what most pro players use. The key is a wide FOV and moderate distance -- you want to see the field without losing track of your car.

## Essential Settings to Change

Out of the box, some defaults are bad for competitive play. Change these immediately:

- **Camera shake** -- Turn it **off**. It adds nothing and obscures your view during impacts.
- **Ball cam toggle** -- Set to **toggle**, not hold. You need ball cam on most of the time and switch off for dribbles and boost grabs.
- **Controller deadzone** -- Lower it to **0.05 - 0.10** for more responsive steering.
- **Dodge deadzone** -- Around **0.70** to prevent accidental flips.
- **Vibration** -- Turn it **off** for cleaner inputs.
- **Display** -- Uncap FPS or match your monitor refresh rate. Input lag matters.

## Improving as a Player

### Training Tools

- **[BakkesMod](/games/mods/bakkesmod)** -- The essential PC plugin. Adds custom training features, ball trajectory prediction, an in-game rank tracker, and the ability to use any cosmetic item. A must-have.
- **Custom Training Packs** -- Community-created drills for specific skills (aerials, redirects, wall shots). Browse popular ones in-game or find codes online.
- **Freeplay** -- Underrated. Just driving around hitting the ball in freeplay builds mechanics faster than most people realize. Focus on hitting the ball hard, recovering quickly, and maintaining speed.

### Key Skills by Rank

**Getting out of Gold/Platinum:**
- Learn basic **rotation** (back post rotation, not double-committing)
- Improve **recovery** speed (wavedashing, flipping to maintain momentum)
- **Power shots** -- hitting the ball hard and on target beats fancy moves

**Getting out of Diamond/Champion:**
- **Fast aerials** (jump + boost simultaneously, then double jump)
- **Backboard defense** and reads
- **Shadow defense** (driving backward while facing the opponent)
- **50/50s** -- winning challenges by reading the opponent

**Advanced mechanics** (Grand Champion+):
- Flip resets, ceiling shots, air dribbles, musty flicks
- These are flashy but not required until high ranks -- fundamentals always come first

### The Golden Rule

Consistency beats mechanics. A player who can put hard shots on target and rotate properly will outperform a player who can hit flip resets but whiffs half the time. Drill the basics until they are automatic.
