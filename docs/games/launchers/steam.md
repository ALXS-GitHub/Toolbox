# Steam

Steam is, by a wide margin, the largest digital storefront for PC games. Developed by Valve, it has grown from a simple game updater into a sprawling platform that touches nearly every aspect of PC gaming: purchasing, downloading, updating, organizing, socializing, modding, streaming, and even hardware.

Your entire game library lives in Steam. Games update automatically, progress syncs across machines through Steam Cloud, and the built-in overlay gives you access to chat, guides, and a web browser without leaving your game. The **Workshop** is where community content thrives -- mods, maps, skins, and custom scenarios that you can subscribe to and have installed automatically. For many games, Workshop support alone is reason enough to buy on Steam over other storefronts.

**Remote Play** lets you stream games from your main PC to other devices on your network (or over the internet), turning a laptop or phone into a gaming terminal. **Big Picture Mode** redesigns the interface for TV screens and controllers, which is also the foundation for the **Steam Deck** experience. On Linux and the Deck, **Proton** -- Valve's compatibility layer -- runs a surprisingly large portion of the Windows game catalog without any configuration on your part.

A few things worth setting up early: enable **Steam Guard** (two-factor authentication) to lock down your account, and visit **Library > Storage Manager** to control where games are installed if you have multiple drives. You can also add non-Steam games to your library through *Games > Add a Non-Steam Game*, which gives them overlay support and lets you launch everything from one place.
