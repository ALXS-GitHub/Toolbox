# ASUS ROG Delta II

This is my current headset, the one I wear for gaming sessions, calls, and music alike. The ROG Delta II solves a problem that most gaming headsets pretend does not exist: the choice between connection modes. Bluetooth headsets work everywhere but add latency. 2.4 GHz wireless dongles give low-latency audio but only connect to one device. Wired headsets eliminate both issues but tether you to your desk. ASUS decided the answer is all three, and then went one further with DualFlow Audio, which lets the headset maintain a 2.4 GHz connection and a Bluetooth connection simultaneously.

In practice, DualFlow means you can be in a game with low-latency audio over 2.4 GHz (via ROG SpeedNova) while taking a phone call or listening to a podcast from your phone over Bluetooth, without touching a single setting. The headset mixes both audio streams in real time. When the call ends, the game audio is still there, uninterrupted. A 3.5 mm wired option rounds out the trio for situations where wireless is not available or battery is dead, though that second scenario is unlikely given the battery life.

## Sound and Battery

The 50 mm drivers use titanium-plated diaphragms, and in 2.4 GHz mode the headset pushes Hi-Res Audio at 24-bit/96 kHz. The sound stage is wide enough to reliably place footsteps in competitive shooters and detailed enough to make music sound like more than an afterthought, which is rarer in gaming headsets than it should be. The D-shaped ear cushions seal well without clamping, and at 318 grams the weight disappears after a few minutes.

Battery life is the headline number: over 110 hours on a single charge in 2.4 GHz mode. That is not a typo, and it is not an "up to" figure achieved by turning everything off. In daily use, this translates to charging the headset roughly once a month. When you do need to charge, 15 minutes on the cable gives back about 11 hours of use, so forgetting to plug it in overnight is never a crisis.

## The Microphone

The detachable boom mic is a 10 mm super-wideband unit that captures voice with enough clarity and bandwidth to be usable for Discord calls, team meetings, and casual streaming without needing a separate desk microphone. It attaches magnetically and comes off cleanly when you want to use the headset as regular headphones out in the world.

Platform compatibility spans PC, Mac, PlayStation 4 and 5, Xbox (via 3.5 mm), and Nintendo Switch. It is the kind of headset that replaces three or four other audio devices by doing all of their jobs well enough that none of them are missed.
