# Akko MOD007 V3 HE — Year of Dragon

This is my current daily keyboard. Most mechanical keyboards register a keypress when a metal leaf physically touches a contact point inside the switch. The MOD007 V3 HE does something fundamentally different: each key sits above a Hall effect sensor that reads the position of a magnet in the switch stem, detecting exactly how far down the key has traveled at any given moment. There is no physical contact to register the press, which means no contact bounce, no debounce delay, and no mechanical wear on the actuation mechanism. The result is a keyboard that can respond to input faster and more precisely than any traditional mechanical switch allows.

## Why Hall Effect Changes the Game

The Akko Cream Yellow Magnetic switches installed in this board let you set the actuation point anywhere between 0.1 mm and 3.4 mm of travel. For gaming, you can dial actuation down to a hair trigger where barely touching the key fires the input. For typing, you can push it deeper so keys feel more deliberate and accidental presses disappear. This is not a one-size-fits-all compromise; it is per-key configurability that traditional switches simply cannot offer.

On top of adjustable actuation, the board supports Rapid Trigger, which resets the key the instant you begin releasing it rather than waiting for the switch to travel back past a fixed reset point. In fast-paced games where counter-strafing or precise movement matters, the difference is immediately noticeable. Dynamic Keystrokes (DKS) take it further by letting a single key fire different actions at different stages of the press: one action when the key passes 1 mm, another at 2 mm, another on release. Snap Key handles SOCD (simultaneous opposing cardinal directions) by automatically resolving conflicts when you press left and right at the same time, outputting the last key pressed rather than canceling both inputs.

The board polls at 8000 Hz, reporting its state to the PC eight thousand times per second. At that rate, the polling interval is 0.125 ms, which effectively removes the keyboard from the latency chain in any scenario where human reaction time is the bottleneck.

## The Object Itself

Beyond the technology, this is a striking piece of hardware. The 75% layout sits inside a CNC-milled aluminum frame finished with a three-tone anodization process in cyan and teal tones, part of Akko's Year of the Dragon collection. Dragon-themed artwork adorns the weight on the back. The keycaps are PBT with 5-sided dye-sublimation printing, so the legends and artwork wrap around the sides of the caps and will not fade from use. A multifunctional knob in the top right corner defaults to volume control but can be reassigned to adjust backlighting, scroll through tabs, or trigger any custom function through the software.

It manages to be both a serious competitive gaming tool and something you actually want sitting on your desk.
