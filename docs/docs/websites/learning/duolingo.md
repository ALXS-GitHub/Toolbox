# Duolingo

**[Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/)** is a free language learning platform that turns vocabulary, grammar, and listening practice into short gamified lessons. It supports over 40 languages, from widely studied ones like Spanish, French, and Japanese to less common options like Hawaiian, Navajo, and High Valyrian. Lessons are bite-sized (typically 2-5 minutes each), which makes it easy to fit practice into a daily routine.

The gamification is what keeps people coming back. Duolingo tracks your daily streak -- how many consecutive days you have completed at least one lesson -- and the streak quickly becomes its own motivation. There are also leagues where you compete weekly against other learners based on XP earned, hearts that limit mistakes on the free plan, and achievements for hitting milestones. The mascot, Duo the owl, sends notifications that have become famous for their increasingly passive-aggressive tone when you skip a day.

Duolingo is free to use with ads and a heart system that limits how many mistakes you can make before waiting. The paid **Super Duolingo** plan removes ads, gives unlimited hearts, adds personalized practice and progress tracking, and unlocks offline lessons. For casual learners, the free tier is more than sufficient. Duolingo is not going to make you fluent on its own -- real conversation practice and immersion are irreplaceable -- but as a daily habit for building vocabulary and reinforcing grammar patterns, it is one of the best options available.
