# YouTube

**[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/)** is the largest video platform on the internet, hosting everything from music and entertainment to deep technical tutorials and conference talks. For developers and learners, it is one of the most valuable free resources available -- if you know how to use it efficiently.

## Keyboard shortcuts

YouTube's player has a full set of keyboard shortcuts that make navigation significantly faster than clicking around. These work when the player is focused:

| Key              | Action                                      |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| `K` or `Space`   | Play / Pause                                |
| `J`              | Rewind 10 seconds                           |
| `L`              | Skip forward 10 seconds                     |
| `Left` / `Right` | Rewind / skip 5 seconds                     |
| `Shift` + `>`    | Increase playback speed                     |
| `Shift` + `<`    | Decrease playback speed                     |
| `F`              | Toggle fullscreen                           |
| `T`              | Toggle theater mode                         |
| `I`              | Toggle miniplayer                           |
| `C`              | Toggle captions                             |
| `M`              | Mute / unmute                               |
| `0`-`9`          | Jump to 0%-90% of video                     |
| `,` / `.`        | Frame-by-frame (when paused)                |

The speed shortcuts (`Shift` + `>` / `<`) are particularly valuable. Watching tutorials at 1.5x or 2x saves enormous amounts of time once you get used to it.

## Recommended channels for tech and learning

YouTube's recommendation algorithm can be a distraction engine, but subscribing to quality channels and using the Subscriptions feed (instead of Home) keeps things focused. Here are some channels worth following:

### Development and programming

- **[Fireship](https://www.youtube.com/@Fireship)** -- short, fast-paced videos on web technologies, frameworks, and industry news. The "100 seconds of X" series is excellent for quick overviews.
- **[ThePrimeagen](https://www.youtube.com/@ThePrimeagen)** -- entertaining takes on programming, performance, Vim, and developer culture. Also has a clips channel.
- **[Theo - t3.gg](https://www.youtube.com/@t3dotgg)** -- web development opinions, TypeScript ecosystem, and tech industry commentary.
- **[NetworkChuck](https://www.youtube.com/@NetworkChuck)** -- networking, cybersecurity, Linux, and homelab content. Beginner-friendly and energetic.
- **[Traversy Media](https://www.youtube.com/@TraversyMedia)** -- comprehensive web development tutorials and crash courses.

### Science and math

- **[3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/@3blue1brown)** -- beautiful animated explanations of mathematics, linear algebra, neural networks, and more. Some of the best educational content on the platform.
- **[Veritasium](https://www.youtube.com/@veritasium)** -- physics, engineering, and science communication.
- **[Computerphile](https://www.youtube.com/@Computerphile)** -- computer science topics explained by academics.

### Productivity and tech culture

- **[Linus Tech Tips](https://www.youtube.com/@LinusTechTips)** -- hardware reviews, PC builds, and tech experiments.
- **[Jeff Geerling](https://www.youtube.com/@JeffGeerling)** -- Raspberry Pi, homelab, Ansible, and self-hosting.

## Getting more out of YouTube

### Use the Subscriptions feed

The Home page is algorithmically curated to maximize watch time, not learning. The **Subscriptions** tab shows a chronological feed of uploads from channels you follow, giving you control over what you see.

### Playlists as courses

Many creators organize tutorial series into playlists that function as free courses. When learning a new technology, look for a playlist rather than individual videos -- the structured progression makes a big difference.

### Playback speed

Defaulting to 1.5x or 2x for tutorials you are already somewhat familiar with is one of the highest-leverage habits you can build. Use 1x for new or complex material, speed up for review and reinforcement.

### Chapters and timestamps

Most well-produced videos include chapter markers visible in the progress bar. You can click directly to the section you need, which makes long tutorial videos far more useful as reference material.

## YouTube Premium

The paid subscription removes all ads (including mid-roll interruptions during tutorials), enables background playback on mobile (audio continues when you switch apps), and allows offline downloads. Whether it is worth the cost depends on how much you use the platform, but for heavy users it removes significant friction. Family plans bring the per-person cost down considerably.
