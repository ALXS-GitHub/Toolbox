# Netflix

**[Netflix](https://www.netflix.com/)** is one of the most widely used streaming platforms, offering a massive library of movies, TV shows, documentaries, and original productions. The desktop experience, whether through a browser or the Windows app, supports multiple profiles so each household member gets personalized recommendations. Netflix lets you adjust streaming quality in your account settings, which is useful for saving bandwidth or forcing the highest available resolution. Subtitles are highly customizable too -- you can change the font size, color, and background directly from the account preferences page, and those settings carry over to every device.

A handful of keyboard shortcuts make browsing more comfortable on desktop. Pressing **Space** pauses or resumes playback, **F** toggles fullscreen, and **M** mutes the audio. The left and right arrow keys skip backward and forward by ten seconds, which is handy for rewinding a line of dialogue or jumping past an intro. For anyone on a slower connection or with limited data, Netflix also offers a download feature in the Windows app, letting you save episodes and movies for offline viewing on the go.

Quality-wise, the plan you subscribe to determines the maximum resolution available, ranging from 480p on the basic tier up to 4K Ultra HD with HDR on the premium tier. If you care about picture quality, it is worth double-checking that your playback settings are not locked to a lower data usage tier, as Netflix defaults to automatic quality which does not always pick the highest option.
