# Zen

**[Zen](https://zen-browser.app/)** is a peaceful and privacy-minded browser based on Firefox. It’s beautifully designed to help you stay focused, not distracted think of it as a breathing space for your browsing.

## Tab & Window Management

- **Sidebar with vertical tabs**  
  Tabs are shown in a tidy vertical sidebar, rather than a cluttered top bar.

- **Workspaces**  
  Keep different projects separate like “Work” vs “Personal” by grouping tabs into switchable workspaces.

- **Split View**  
  View two or more web pages side by side in the same window for easy multitasking.

- **Glance**  
  Hold a key (`alt`) and click a link to open a quick preview in a small modal window no need to create a full tab.

## Syncing

Zen makes it easy to take your browsing setup anywhere. With a Zen account, you can:

- Sync your open tabs across devices.  
- Keep your settings, workspaces, and themes consistent.  
- Start browsing on one computer and continue seamlessly on another.  

## Personal Touch & Appearance

- **Zen Mods (Themes Store)**  
  Customize how Zen looks using community-made themes add color, textures, and different moods.

## Privacy & Security

- **Built on Firefox**  
  Zen supports Firefox extensions and runs on the secure Gecko engine.

- **Tracking protection**  
  Enhanced tracking protection is built-in, and no extra user data is collected.

## Platforms & Compatibility

- **Available on Windows, macOS, and Linux**  
  Downloadable for all major desktop systems, with multiple installation options.

## What to Expect

- **Clean and calming UI**  
  Minimal and intuitive layout made for ease and focus.

- **Still evolving**  
  Some features like Split View can feel a bit experimental, but improvements are ongoing.

- **Streaming limitations**  
  DRM content (like Netflix) isn’t yet supported, though work is in progress.

## Community Buzz

- Many users say Zen feels like a breath of fresh air beautiful, focused, and open-source.  
- Positive feedback highlights its clean design and customization, though a few advanced features are still catching up.
